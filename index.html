Every year, the Mar Gregorios Orthodox Christian Student Movement (MGOCSM) of North America hosts Leadership Camp through the blessings of our Metropolitans and by the Grace of God. Leadership Camp gives our youth the opportunity to not only meet others across North America who will help them in their spiritual journey, but also the opportunity to become and grow as the leaders and future of the Orthodox Church.

Leadership Camp brings together representatives from MGOCSM units all across North America, from California to Virginia, and Canada to Texas. It trains them in a four-day period to create a new generation of leaders within the Malankara Orthodox Community.

Participants are selected by their parishes because they are individuals who actively inspire the Faith through worshipping our Lord, studying His Word, and serving His calling. These representatives will attend Leadership Camp on behalf of their MGOCSMs and by the end, we hope they learn to inspire and lead the youth of their churches and areas to the Church and grow in our Faith.

The New York region has been blessed with a passionate MGOCSM, comprised of a total of 26 churches. With the growing bonds between these churches, the love and passion we share for the Orthodox faith, and the desire to continue growing and spreading our Faith, New York has been blessed with the opportunity to host the 15th Annual Leadership Camp for the year 2015. The theme of New York Leadership Camp (NYLC) 2015 is Rising to His Glory. This theme is based on Colossians 3:1: “If then you were raised with Christ, seek those things which are above, where Christ is, sitting on the right hand of God.” Our committee hopes and prays that each participant will be able to resonate with this theme and inspire those back home with the tools and experiences gained at Leadership Camp.

New York Leadership Camp 2015 will be held from July 22nd to July 25th, and is expecting around 120 participants, including delegates, non-delegates, speakers, and clergy. We are looking forward to NYLC 2015 and would like everyone to keep the committee, the Leadership Camp, its future participants, and its aspired success in their prayers.
